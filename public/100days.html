100 Days of UI

In order to practice my visual design skills, I recently took up the challenge to make 100 UI design assets over the course of 100 days.

Roles

User Experience, Visual Design, Interaction Design, Branding

Duration

March 2021 - Present

Overview

I took this challenge with one goal in mind: to get better. I feel like it's easy to get comfortable in almost anything. In order to avoid comfort I try to take challenges like this one, often, in hopes that it becomes another opportunity for me to be better than I was yesterday.

Recently I've been inspired to "show up" everyday and create luck for myself by doing so. This excercise is also an opportunity for me to get outside my comfort zone by posting my work for others to see. So far it's been rewarding. And I can't wait to continue my journey as a young and developing designer.

I've surfaced a few of my favorite moments from thee challenge but you can keep up with my entire 100 Days of UI journey on Dribbble.

Context

Each day designers are given a prompt. I frame each solution clearly identitfying the prompt and my thought process in getting to the solution. I also show my designs in order from my earliest design to my most recent.

Day 1 - Sign Up

Prompt:
"Create a sign up page, modal, form, or app screen related to signing up for something. It could be for a volunteer event, contest registration, a giveaway, or anything you can image."

Solution:
I wanted to go for a basic sign up page for any app. I was focused on making sure all of the necessary fields were accounted for and then just keeping good design fundamentals. I also like to use an 8pt grid for spacing so you'll be seeing a lot more of that haha.

Day 2 - Credit Card Checkout

Prompt:

"Design a credit card checkout form or page. Don't forget the important elements such as the numbers, dates, security numbers, etc."

Solution:

Other than an exercise in spacing, type, and making sure all fields were accounted for in the checkout process. I wanted to practice my dark UI capabilities and wanted to make sure I was mindful of current quick checkout methods as well as applying discount codes. My thought for the discount code is the button will light up like the confirm purchase one when the coupon code is approved and you may then select it. The page will refresh and a new line will go before total applying the discount.

Day 03 - Landing Page

Prompt:

"Design a Landing Page
--
What's the main focus? Is it for a book, an album, a mobile app, a product? Consider important landing page elements (call-to-actions, clarity, etc.)"

Solution:

Initially, I didn't want to do this challenge. I mean if you're anything like me you've made 10000000 landing pages and you're kind of over it. But then I decided well I don't have to make a typical one. So that's exactly what I did...I went for a landing page that didn't have a typical look to it and I'm happy with how it came out.

Ultimately landing pages are about effectiveness, right? So the challenge here was making something atypical while remaining effective. Granted, we'd have to do some testing to tell whether my landing page is still effective but I'm generally happy with it.

Day 10 - Social Share

Prompt:

"Design a social share button/icon and be mindful of the size, imagery, placement, and purpose for sharing."

Solution:

Today I wanted to take a step back and make a super-refined and less abstract design that reflected product at the highest level, as it stands today. I'm really happy with this. For context this shot is a view from when the user clicks the copy button.

Day 11 - Flash Message

Prompt:

"Design a Flash Message with both the outcome for an error and success. Is it for a sign up form? A download/upload message?"

Solution:

I think the simple parts of product design we often take for granted are actually opportunities for us to explore as designers and come up with something potentially really fun. With the challenge today, I wanted to ensure I held that mindset.

I took a fun approach to flash messages for a hypothetical design->client submission portal. I wanted to make sure there wasn't just a single button on the page because I feel like sometimes, I want to quickly submit something again when I'm in similar submission portals and I have to navigate back a page, OR TWO, to submit again. Moreover, I know that not all failure notifications are triggered because of user-error and wanted to have an easy way to contact support when users felt that they were infallible.

Little dark mode toggle for my first dabbles in Figma Smart Animation
