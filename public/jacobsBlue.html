Jacob's Blue

Digital storytelling experience, website, and home base for all things Jacob's Blue, recording artist, songwriter and producer.


6 min read
Roles

UI/UX Designer, Front-End Developer, Creative Director, Artist

Duration

December 2020 - January 2021

Step 1: Background and Overview


Background

Jacob's Blue is a recording artist, producer, musician, and songwriter. Before this project there was no home base for Jacob's Blue other than an instagram page. An important aspect to his storytelling style is not just delivering an auditory experience but building a world from which the music he makes comes from and which his audience can find themselves. I was tasked with designing, creative directing, and developing the website. An important note is that I am Jacob's Blue lol.

See the live site, here.

Walter Chrysler

I like to build things, I like to do things. I am having a lot of fun.

Overview

In addition to being the new home for Jacob's Blue, where users should be able to easily navigate to find all things related to the artist. The website was also meant to emphasize and tell the story of his latest release and debut album "Born in the Sunset: Thoughts to Oasis." Due to limited resources the website became the main delivery tool for additional storytelling and needed to become an immersive experience for those listening to the project, fans both returning and new.

That being said time was also an important constraint. The album was already submitted to streaming services with a 8 day window before it went live. That meant the site had to accomplish several things within and 8 day window.

My checklist

  • bring to life vision of Jacob's Blue
  • make sure users could navigate to the site from various funnels easily
  • ensure users could easily explore the story the site/album was telling
  • ensure users could listen to the music and/or easily naviate to their streaming platform of choice
  • build the site to scale both on mobile and desktop
  • accomplish all in 8 days

Step 2: Define the Problem and Goal


Problem

Users don't know the story and haven't heard the music of Born in the Sunset: Thoughts to Oasis by Jacob's Blue. They need to be able to find out more, explore the world he's created, and most importantly play his music. Two important constraints are time, and the fact that most users will have never heard of Jacob's Blue.

Goal

Tell the story of Born in the Sunset: Thoughts to Oasis in an immersive way that doesn't get in the way of helping users navigate to the music quickly and effectively from any device.

User Value

For both current fans and new site visitors to be able to find music easily. And for all who wish, current fan or not, to be able to explore the full storytelling experience.

Step 3: Scope

In the world of an up and coming artist often times the best solution is to do it yourself. As a lean team (me, myself, and I) a lot rested on my shoulders to complete in a short time.


  • Visual Design
  • UX Design
  • Wireframes
  • User Flows
  • Prototype
  • Development (HTML, CSS, JS)
  • Creative Direction

However, after spending a year and a half on the music, one more week of grind didn't seem to bad. It was actually really fun.

Step 4: Target Audience

The audience for Jacob's Blue was already defined. For context, the majority of Jacob's Blue listeners were in one place already. Instagram. That meant that I would have one link in bio directing all users to one place. I needed users familiar with Jacob's Blue to be able to navigate to the link and intuitively, find what they needed, but if they wanted ,to also be able to embrace the storytelling experience laid out before them.

In addition to familiar fans, I knew this site and project would serve as the introduction for many new listeners to Jacob's Blue. It was important that I make it very simple for those users to also navigate the site and find their way through the content.

Don Norman

Knowing how someone will use something is essential.

Step 5: Process

Because of the tight deadline, I rushed a lot of this process. I needed to optimize for all to design objectives and user needs in the quickest and most effective way possible. I was more or less thinking MVP the entire time.

That being said, several aspects came together that made this project successful both from a design and a development standpoint.

Mobile First

To reiterate, the majority of people navigating to this site would be coming from Instagram, Jacob's Blue's main audience base. Factoring in this information and my need for speed in this production, I decided to design a mobile first single column layout. This made development incredibly straightforward as I only needed 1 breakpoint to accommodate all styles.

Sticky and Accesible Nav

A principle I try my best to maintain, especially in an instance like this where user traction is low, is that a navigation menu shouldn't be hidden until they have to be. Users may not even know who Jacob's Blue is yet, so if they come here and can't find what they're looking for immediately it most likely means they won't be listening at all. Both in the mobile and desktop experiences the nav is not hidden and is intentionally sticky so that at any point in the users' experience they're able to get what they ultimately came for, the music.

An important piece of context is that most artists use a Linktree in their link in bio on their social accounts. Linktree is more or less a landing page that houses links to everything relevant to that artist. It's an amazing tool for artist, thus it's popularity has risen recently. I had to assume users were used to this experience more than a website they had never been too.

In order to combat this, I made sure there was a button that leads users to my own linktree where users could navigate to every streaming service humanly imaginable, both in the sticky nav and hero banner.

Surfaced in the nav other than the linktree button, are the most popular streaming services for users convenience so that users would be at least 2 clicks away from instagram to streaming service and at most 3 clicks away.

Personal Touches

The creation of the album was a personal process and I wanted to bring users into that world. As part of the enhancements I added to the desktop experience I added handwritten notes as vector graphics throughout, so that users almost felt like they were in the book, from which I created the entire album.

All my ideas start on paper so it felt like a natural thing to include. Moreover, I think it adds a lot of value to the desktop experience, making it charming for those that venture to explore it.

Immersive Script

Part of the process for this album was the production of an accompanying script. The album and script are meant to be packaged and read together so I wanted to create an environment where people could both read and listen easily. When users navigate to the script page, there is the script itself, a Spotify web-player embed for playing the album, and the ability to download the script for easier reading.

Step 6: Deliverables and Outcome

Overall, considering the timeline or not, this project was wildly successful. It strongly exceeded my expectations. As I hypothesized early in the project, the vast majority of users viewed the site from a mobile platform. Around 84% of users viewed Jacobs.blue on mobile. The site handled almost 1000 unique visitors with no bugs or crashes. The site and album have received quality press from more than 5 other websites. And qualitatively, I received over 60 personal messages from people speaking about their fondness of the site and album.

Step 7: Lessons Learned

I wouldn't change anything about the design at the moment but one thing that bothered me quite a bit is that there is a small delay upon loading the page. It's the not the biggest deal in the world but there are definitely a lot of large images that I can either downsize or use a compression engine in the future to optimize page loading and site performance.

But that's it. I look forward to more challenges, tight windows, and album rollouts like this in the future. Thanks for being here and if you haven't already, check out the site and let me know what you think! Peace ✌🏼
